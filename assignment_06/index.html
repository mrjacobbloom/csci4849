About this app

Instructions

This is a one-button scanning interface like we saw in class. Use any key to choose a row, then to choose a column or cancel and go back to row selection.

Test Results

I decided to test whether the length of the time interval has any real effect of error rate and input speed. I timed myself entering 3 equations into the calculator with interval times 250ms and 500ms. Here are my results:

Interval (ms) Equation Errors Completion time (secs)
250 76+81= 5 57.95
250 34+6= 3 35.42
250 12/6+9= 3 34.29
500 76+81= 1 17.49
500 34+6= 0 17.50
500 12/6+9= 0 21.12

I was able to achieve far more accurate results in less total time with the longer interval length; however, I seemed to get more comfortable with the high-speed scanning as I went, so a power-user might be able to achieve fairly high accuracy at this kind of speed with a little practice.

One thing that struck me was that it was much harder to choose the first and last items in a given row than ones in the middle at the higher speed because the jump to the beginning of the row is disorienting. One direction for further experimentation would be something that alleviates this. For example, a change in the scanning speed as you near the edges, or a scanner that bounces back and forth instead of wrapping around could both help with this.
